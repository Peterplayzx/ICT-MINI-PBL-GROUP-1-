All About Scams

Learn how to identify and protect yourself from various online scams.

What are Scams?

Scams are deceptive schemes designed to trick individuals into giving away personal information, money, or valuables. They often occur through fraudulent emails, websites, phone calls, or text messages that seem legitimate at first glance.

Common Types of Scams

  • Email Scams: Fraudulent emails that appear to come from trusted sources, often with fake links or attachments.
  • Tech Support Scams: Fraudsters posing as tech support agents to trick you into paying for unnecessary services or software.
  • Online Shopping Scams: Fake websites or sellers offering goods that don't exist or are never delivered.
  • Lottery Scams: False promises of lottery winnings or sweepstakes that require payment upfront.
  • Romance Scams: Fraudsters who build fake online relationships to exploit victims emotionally and financially.

How to Recognize Scams

Here are common warning signs of a scam:

  • Unexpected messages asking for money or personal information.
  • Unusual email or phone numbers that don't match legitimate organizations.
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers, like massive discounts or free giveaways.
  • Urgency in requests, pressuring you to act quickly or face consequences.
  • Suspicious links or attachments that seem out of place.

Tips for Staying Safe from Scams

  • Do not share personal or financial information with unknown sources.
  • Always verify the legitimacy of emails, calls, or messages.
  • Check for secure website URLs (https://) before making purchases.
  • Report any suspicious activity to authorities or relevant platforms.
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.

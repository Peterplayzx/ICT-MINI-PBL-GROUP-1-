All About Identity Theft

Learn how to recognize and protect yourself from identity theft attacks.

What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft is a crime where someone illegally obtains and uses another person's personal information, typically for financial gain or to commit fraud. It can involve stealing personal data such as names, Social Security numbers, bank account details, or credit card information to impersonate the victim and make unauthorized purchases or open accounts in their name.

Types of Identity Theft

  • Financial Identity Theft: This involves stealing credit card information, bank accounts, or other financial data to make fraudulent transactions.
  • Medical Identity Theft: Involves using someone else's personal information to obtain medical care or prescriptions.
  • Child Identity Theft: Using a child’s identity to open accounts, commit fraud, or apply for loans.
  • Tax Identity Theft: Fraudsters using your personal details to file false tax returns and claim refunds.
  • Criminal Identity Theft: Someone uses your identity when arrested, leading to potential criminal records in your name.

How to Recognize Identity Theft

If you notice any of these signs, you may be a victim of identity theft:

  • Unexpected charges or withdrawals from your bank account.
  • Not receiving expected bills or statements, which could indicate someone has changed your billing address.
  • Receiving unfamiliar credit cards or loans in your name.
  • Denied credit or being offered high-interest rates because of poor credit history, which is not yours.
  • Finding unfamiliar accounts on your credit report or receiving notices about debts you didn’t incur.

Tips for Protecting Yourself from Identity Theft

  • Shred documents that contain personal information before discarding them.
  • Regularly monitor your bank accounts and credit reports for any suspicious activity.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for your online accounts and avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.
  • Be cautious about sharing personal information online, especially on social media.
  • Place a fraud alert or freeze on your credit report to make it more difficult for criminals to open accounts in your name.
